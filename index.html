Inferno - Dante

Introduction

Dante Alighieri's "Inferno" is the first part of his epic poem "Divine Comedy," one of the greatest literary works in Western literature. Written in the early 14th century, the poem details the journey of Dante through Hell, guided by the Roman poet Virgil...

Stages of Hell

"Inferno" is structured around the concept of nine circles of Hell, each representing a different type of sin...

  1. First Circle - Limbo: The sin of unbaptized souls and virtuous pagans.
  2. Second Circle - Lust: The souls of the lustful are blown about by an eternal storm.
  3. Third Circle - Gluttony: The gluttonous are forced to lie in a filthy, freezing rain.

Characters

The main characters in "Inferno" are Dante, the narrator and protagonist, and his guide, Virgil...

  • Character 1: Francesca da Rimini
    Francesca is a woman from the 13th century who is condemned to the second circle of Hell...
  • Character 2: Count Ugolino
    Count Ugolino is a traitor who is punished in the ninth circle of Hell...

Summary

"Inferno" tells the story of Dante's journey through Hell, a descent that symbolizes his search for understanding...
